story:
  title: "Reconnecting Through Music"
  content: |
    A care home approached me to support a resident living with advanced
    dementia who required one-to-one, 24-hour care. Her needs were complex, and
    although her husband also lived within the same care home, he did not
    require support. Over time, they were no longer able to share a room.

    As her condition progressed, she stopped recognising her husband. Visits
    often led to distress and agitation, which became deeply upsetting for him,
    and eventually he was unable to continue visiting.

    The emotional impact on him and the family was significant, as they grieved
    the growing loss of connection between a couple who had shared their lives
    together.

    From the outset, I worked closely with both the family and the care home
    team, taking time to understand personal history, routines, preferences, and
    existing care plans, always working in line with policies and regulations. I
    carefully selected a small, dedicated team whose skills and emotional
    awareness were the right fit, ensuring every detail was considered.

    Through close supervision, consistent communication with the carers and care
    home team, and ongoing feedback, we began to notice small but meaningful
    moments of calm. One carer identified that music from her past helped her
    settle and feel more at ease. These moments, though brief, were very
    significant.

    Working collaboratively with the care home, I suggested gently
    reintroducing her husband during these calmer periods. With careful planning
    and sensitivity, short visits were trialed. Over time, these became a weekly
    routine.

    Sitting together, sharing tea, smiling, and reminiscing, the couple were
    able to reconnect in a way that felt safe, dignified, and deeply meaningful.
    The joy it brought not only to her husband but their family also — to watch
    your parents be together after such a long struggle, even for short moments,
    was very precious.

    This experience reflects how I work every day: by listening closely, valuing
    relationships, and paying attention to the smallest details that can lead to
    the most meaningful outcomes. High-quality care is not just about meeting
    needs, it is about understanding the person, supporting families, and
    creating moments that truly matter.

    — Charmaine Jennings
