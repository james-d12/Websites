---
title: The Power of Connection
---

I was asked to support a gentleman living in a care home who, although not formally diagnosed, was experiencing significant distress that at times presented as behaviours that challenged those around him. His needs were complex and unpredictable, and this had become increasingly difficult for staff and other residents, highlighting the need for additional, person-centred support within the home.

From the outset, I spent time speaking with his sister to understand him beyond his circumstances — his personality, history, and interests. It became clear he had a strong passion for cars and motorbikes, which shaped my approach. Rather than assigning support based on availability, I carefully matched him with two male carers who shared these interests, believing that genuine connection must feel natural and meaningful.

From the beginning, there was a noticeable shift. Trust began to build through shared conversations, and he became increasingly settled, calm, and responsive. This not only supported his wellbeing but also significantly benefited the care home environment. Staff reported a reduction in distressing incidents, and the overall atmosphere within the home became more peaceful and manageable, easing pressure on both staff and other residents.

This experience reinforced a core belief in my practice: behaviour is a form of communication, and with the right understanding and connection, positive change is possible. By looking beyond the surface and valuing individuality, we can create the right conditions for people to feel safe, understood, and supported.

Because sometimes, it is not just about care — it is about connection.

— Charmaine Jennings
