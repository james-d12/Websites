---
title: Regaining Confidence After Surgery
---

I was approached by a family whose loved one had recently been discharged from hospital following a hip replacement.
Like many families in this situation, they were understandably concerned about her safety, mobility, and how she would
manage during the early stages of recovery.

Transitions from hospital to care home can be a particularly vulnerable time. My focus is always to ensure the right
level of care and support is in place, while also encouraging independence and confidence as recovery progresses, whilst
following the care plan.

After taking the time to understand her needs, personality, and pace, I carefully selected a carer with the right
balance of experience, patience, and encouragement.

From the outset, the approach was gentle and consistent. With the right guidance and reassurance, she began to regain
confidence in her movement, with each day bringing meaningful progress. Through one-to-one support, she rebuilt her
strength safely and steadily, and soon she was moving independently and confidently once again.

Importantly, we also recognised when the level of support was no longer required in the same capacity. The care period
proved shorter than initially expected, reflecting both her determination and the effectiveness of the tailored
approach.

— Charmaine Jennings
